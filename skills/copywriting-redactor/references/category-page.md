# Category Pages

**Stage:** TOFU → MOFU
**Objective:** Help users navigate and discover products or services within a specific category. Simultaneously, serve as a high-authority SEO hub for the category keyword, driving organic traffic and distributing link equity to individual product/service pages.

---

## Guiding Principle

A category page is not a blank grid of product cards. It's an SEO asset and a navigation tool. The copy gives Google enough context to rank the page for the category keyword, and gives the visitor enough context to find what they need without friction.

**The balance:** Enough text to rank, not so much that it buries the products. Copy supports discovery — it doesn't compete with it.

---

## Recommended Structure

### 1. Category Headline + Intro
- **H1:** Contains the primary category keyword. Clear and descriptive.
- **Intro paragraph:** 2-4 sentences that describe the category, who it's for, and what differentiates your selection.
- Place above the product/service grid so it's the first indexable content.

❌ "Shoes"
✅ "Running Shoes for Every Terrain — Trail, Road, and Track"

❌ "Our Products"
✅ "Project Management Tools Built for Remote Teams"

### 2. Subcategory Navigation (if applicable)
- If the category is broad, provide quick-filter links or subcategory cards.
- Each subcategory link should use descriptive anchor text (not "View All").
- This creates internal linking pathways and helps users narrow down faster.

### 3. Product/Service Grid
- The copy here is minimal but intentional:
  - **Product name:** Clear and keyword-aware
  - **Short descriptor:** 1 line — what it is or who it's for
  - **Price** (if applicable)
  - **CTA:** "View Details", "Compare", "Add to Cart"

- Let the grid be clean. The heavy copy goes above and below it.

### 4. Category Description (below the grid)
- 200-500 words of substantive SEO content about the category.
- Cover:
  - What the category includes and why it matters
  - How to choose the right option (brief buying guide)
  - Key differentiators of your brand's offerings in this category
  - Internal links to related categories, guides, or blog posts

- Break into 2-3 subsections with H2/H3 headings for scannability.
- **Do NOT keyword-stuff.** Write for the reader; the keyword placement should feel natural.

### 5. FAQ Section (optional but high SEO value)
- 3-5 common questions about the category.
- Targets long-tail and question-based keywords.
- Use FAQ schema markup for rich snippet eligibility.

---

## SEO for Category Pages

Category pages are high-priority SEO assets. They target broad, high-volume keywords and distribute authority to child pages.

- **Target keywords:** "[category name]", "[category] for [audience]", "best [category]", "[category] online"
- **Title tag:** `[Category Name] | [Brand]` or `[Category Name] — [Differentiator] | [Brand]`
- **H1:** Exact or close-match category keyword
- **Meta description:** What the category includes + number of products/options + CTA. 150-160 characters.
- **URL:** `/category-name/` or `/products/category-name/` — short, keyword-rich, no parameters
- **Internal linking:** Link to top child pages, related categories, and supporting blog content. Category pages should be in the main navigation and linked from the homepage.
- **Schema markup:** ItemList schema for product grids. FAQ schema if FAQ section is present.
- **Pagination:** If the category spans multiple pages, use rel=next/prev and ensure the first page has the intro + category description.
- **Minimum copy:** 300-500 words of unique, descriptive content. Avoid thin category pages.

---

## Adaptations by Category Type

| Category Type | Copy Emphasis |
|---|---|
| Ecommerce product category | Buying guide angle, filter guidance, price ranges |
| Service category | Who each service is for, comparison matrix, trust signals |
| Content/resource category | Topic overview, reading path recommendations, expertise signals |
| B2B solution category | Use-case segmentation, industry-specific language, ROI framing |

---

## Anti-patterns to Avoid

- ❌ No copy at all — just a grid of products (Google sees a thin page)
- ❌ Giant wall of SEO text above the products that pushes them below the fold
- ❌ Duplicate category descriptions across similar pages
- ❌ "Welcome to our [Category] section" as the intro
- ❌ Keyword-stuffed paragraphs that read like they were written for a bot
- ❌ No internal links to or from the category page
- ❌ Missing subcategory navigation on broad categories
- ❌ Pagination that orphans important products from the indexed page
