# Copywriting Formulas — Complete Reference

This file centralizes all copywriting formulas with their description, when to use them, when NOT to use them, and an applied example. **Always combine the formula with brand voice** — the formula is the skeleton; the voice is what makes it human.

## 1. AIDA
**Attention → Interest → Desire → Action**

The classic from David Ogilvy and Claude Hopkins. Four mental states the reader must go through in order to reach action.

| Phase | What it does | Example copy |
|---|---|---|
| **Attention** | Stops the scroll. Creates curiosity or names a pain. | "You've been paying for a tool your team hates using for 3 years." |
| **Interest** | Expands the problem or presents the context that makes it relevant. | "The average B2B software adoption rate is 43%. The rest gets ignored." |
| **Desire** | Paints the transformation. The reader imagines themselves with the problem solved. | "Imagine a system your team opens on their own, without anyone telling them to." |
| **Action** | Clear, specific CTA with minimal friction. | "Start your 14-day trial — no credit card required." |

**Use it for:** Landing pages, sales pages, conversion emails, direct response ads.
**Don't use it for:** TOFU educational content, informational blog articles (the reader isn't in buying mode).
**Common mistake:** Jumping to Desire without building Interest. The reader says "so what?" and leaves.

## 2. PAS
**Problem → Agitation → Solution**

Created by Dan Kennedy. The most direct and most effective in markets with acute pains. It works because the reader first feels understood, then that the situation is urgent, then that there's a way out.

| Phase | What it does | Example copy |
|---|---|---|
| **Problem** | Names the problem using the buyer persona's own words. | "Your pipeline is full but close rates stay the same." |
| **Agitation** | Shows the cost of not solving the problem. Emotional + rational. | "Every month without improving your close rate is another month handing clients to the competition. And your team knows it." |
| **Solution** | Introduces the product as the natural and logical way out. | "That's why we built [X]: a sales process your reps will actually use because it works on the first call." |

**Use it for:** Headlines, landing page intros, reactivation emails, ads for well-defined problems.
**Don't use it for:** Audiences that don't recognize the problem yet (cold TOFU). Educate before you agitate.
**Common mistake:** Agitating without resolving. Leaving the reader anxious with no way out generates rejection, not conversion.

---

## 3. PASO
**Problem → Agitation → Solution → Outcome**

Variant of PAS that adds the final result. More effective when the product's benefit isn't obvious or when ROI is the main selling argument.

| Phase | Example copy |
|---|---|
| **Problem** | "You're managing campaigns across 4 different platforms and never know which one works." |
| **Agitation** | "60% of B2B ad budget is wasted on channels that don't convert. And without unified visibility, you'll keep guessing." |
| **Solution** | "[X] connects all your platforms into a single dashboard with real attribution." |
| **Outcome** | "Our clients reduce CPA by an average of 34% in the first 60 days." |

**Use it for:** Sales pages with high price points, proposals, demos, MOFU content where the outcome justifies the investment.

## 4. BAB — Before–After–Bridge

Very visual. Creates contrast between the current state (pain) and the desired state (transformation), then presents the product as the bridge between the two.

| Phase | Example copy |
|---|---|
| **Before** | "Every Monday you start with three spreadsheets, two chats, and a meeting just to know where your team stands." |
| **After** | "Imagine arriving on Monday and seeing in 30 seconds what's blocked, what's on track, and what needs your attention today." |
| **Bridge** | "[X] is that dashboard. In real time. Without follow-up meetings." |

**Use it for:** Landing page intros, home page heroes, nurturing emails, awareness ads with a known product.
**Advantage over PAS:** More aspirational and less pessimistic. Works better with audiences that already have the problem identified but not urgent.

## 5. FAB — Feature → Advantage → Benefit

Not a full structure formula — it's a translation technique for each product feature. Converts specifications into reasons to buy.

```
[Feature] → what the product has/does
[Advantage] → why that's better than the alternative
[Benefit] → what that means in the buyer's real life
```

| Feature | Advantage | Benefit |
|---|---|---|
| End-to-end encryption | No one else can read the data, even in transit | You comply with GDPR without needing a lawyer for every integration |
| Offline sync | Works without internet | Your field team doesn't lose data when there's no signal |
| Open API | Integrates with any stack | You don't change your current tools to use ours |

**Use it for:** Product pages, feature sections in landing pages, commercial proposals.
**Common mistake:** Stopping at the Feature or the Advantage without reaching the Benefit. Buyers don't buy features; they buy what features do for them.

## 6. 4Ps — Promise → Picture → Proof → Push

Created by Henry Hoke. More narrative than AIDA. Works especially well in long-form formats.

| Phase | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Promise** | The product's central promise. Specific and credible. | "Close more deals without growing your sales team." |
| **Picture** | Paints what life looks like with that promise fulfilled. | "Your team reaches end of month having exceeded target for the first time in two years. Without overtime." |
| **Proof** | Evidence that makes the promise credible. Data, cases, testimonials. | "Company X went from 18% to 31% close rate in Q1 2024. Without hiring a single rep." |
| **Push** | CTA with urgency or reason to act now. | "Q2 implementations are almost full. Book your demo this week." |

**Use it for:** Long sales pages, sales emails, formal proposals.

## 7. The 4Us — For Headlines and Subject Lines

Created by Michael Masterson (AWAI). Every headline or subject line should score high on at least 3 of these 4 criteria:

| U | Question | Strong example | Weak example |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Urgent** | Why act now? | "Before June 30: switch your plan with no penalty" | "Upgrade your plan today" |
| **Unique** | Does it say something no one else says? | "The only CRM that trains itself on your own sales calls" | "The best CRM on the market" |
| **Ultra-specific** | Does it have a number or concrete detail? | "Cut onboarding time from 3 weeks to 4 days" | "Faster onboarding" |
| **Useful** | Does the reader care? | "Stop losing leads to late follow-up" | "Automate your sales" |

**Use it for:** Evaluating and improving any headline before publishing. Score 1-4 and rewrite if below 3.

## 8. QUEST
**Qualify → Understand → Educate → Stimulate → Transition**

For long-form content where the reader needs to be convinced gradually. Very useful in markets where the product requires a mindset shift.

| Phase | What it does |
|---|---|
| **Qualify** | Confirms who it's for: filters the right reader from the start |
| **Understand** | Demonstrates that you understand their situation better than they can describe it |
| **Educate** | Teaches something new that reframes the problem or the solution |
| **Stimulate** | Generates desire by showing the possible result |
| **Transition** | Takes the reader to the next natural step (CTA) |

**Use it for:** Sales pages for complex products, lead-generating whitepapers, conversion webinars.

---

## 9. SLAP
**Stop → Look → Act → Purchase**

Designed for short, high-speed copy. Each element must be completed in seconds, not minutes.

| Phase | Target duration | Application |
|---|---|---|
| **Stop** | 0.5 seconds | The visual hook or first line that stops the scroll |
| **Look** | 3-5 seconds | The subheadline or image that confirms it's worth reading |
| **Act** | 10-15 seconds | Enough copy to understand the value proposition |
| **Purchase** | 1 click | The visible, clear, frictionless CTA |

**Use it for:** Social media ads, banners, single-CTA emails, landing page hero sections.

---

## 10. APP — Agree → Promise → Preview

Developed by Brian Dean for content intros. Very effective for blog articles and nurturing emails.

| Phase | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Agree** | Start by validating something the reader already believes | "SEO in 2025 is harder than it was 3 years ago. That's not an excuse — it's a fact." |
| **Promise** | Promise they'll learn or achieve something concrete | "In this article you'll see exactly which strategies still work and why." |
| **Preview** | Preview the content without spoiling everything | "Includes 3 tactics that don't require backlinks or extra budget." |

**Use it for:** Blog intros (especially MOFU), nurturing emails, video intros.
**Why it works:** The Agree reduces initial resistance. The reader feels the writer is on their side.

---

## 11. Star–Story–Solution

For narrative copy. The product solves the problem of someone real (or representative).

| Phase | Description |
|---|---|
| **Star** | Introduce the protagonist — who is the buyer persona, not the brand |
| **Story** | Tell the conflict they faced (with specific, real details) |
| **Solution** | Introduce the product as the solution that changed the story |

**Compressed example:**
> *Ana led a team of 12 consultants. Each project had its own spreadsheet, its own WhatsApp group, its own tracking system. Three projects were delivered late in one quarter. She implemented [X]. The next quarter: zero delays, same workload.*

**Use it for:** Amplified testimonials, case study sections, reactivation emails, About pages.

---

## 12. PPPP — Problem → Promise → Proof → Proposal

More commercial variant of the 4Ps. Useful when price is the main objection.

| Phase | Example |
|---|---|
| **Problem** | "80% of software projects are delivered behind schedule." |
| **Promise** | "Our methodology guarantees on-time delivery or we refund 20%." |
| **Proof** | "94 projects delivered. 91 on time. The remaining 3 triggered the guarantee." |
| **Proposal** | "Book a free diagnostic session. No commitment, with a project plan at the end." |

## Quick Selection Table

| Situation | Recommended formula |
|---|---|
| BOFU landing page, clear pain | PAS or PASO |
| BOFU landing page, aspirational | BAB or 4Ps |
| Long sales page, high price | AIDA or QUEST |
| TOFU informational blog | APP + heading structure |
| MOFU comparative blog | BAB + FAB per alternative |
| Ecommerce product page | FAB + Star–Story–Solution |
| Pricing page | PPPP or 4Ps |
| Headline / subject line | Evaluate with the 4Us |
| Short ad / banner | SLAP |
| Nurturing email | APP or BAB |
| Case study / testimonial | Star–Story–Solution |
| Technical feature copy | FAB (always) |
