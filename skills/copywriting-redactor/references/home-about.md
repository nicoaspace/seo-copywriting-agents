# Home Page & About Page

---

## HOME PAGE

**Stage:** TOFU → MOFU
**Objective:** In 5 seconds, the visitor must know what you do, for whom, and why they should care. In 30 seconds, they should want to know more.

### Guiding Principle

The home doesn't sell. The home directs. Its job is to take the right visitor to the right page, with enough confidence to keep exploring.

**The 5-second test:** Show the home to someone who doesn't know the brand. In 5 seconds they should be able to answer: What does this company do? For whom? Why should I care?

---

### Recommended Structure — Home Page

#### 1. Hero Section
- **Headline:** What you do + for whom + the result. Not the company name.
- **Subheadline:** How you do it or what makes you different. 1-2 lines maximum.
- **Primary CTA:** The most valuable action the visitor can take now.
- **Secondary CTA:** For those who aren't ready yet (e.g.: "See how it works").

**Technique:** Clarity over cleverness. A clever headline that isn't understood loses to a clear one that is.

#### 2. Trust Bar (quick social proof)
- Client logos, media mentions, awards, or number of users.
- Goes right after the hero, before the visitor scrolls down.

#### 3. Expanded Value Proposition
- 3 pillars or key differentiators.
- Format: icon + title + 1-2 lines of description.
- Each pillar speaks to a benefit, not a feature.

#### 4. How It Works (optional but powerful)
- 3 simple steps. Visitors want to know the "what now?" before they commit.
- Use action verbs in the title of each step.

#### 5. Case Studies or Testimonials
- 1-3 brief stories with a concrete result.
- Real photo + full name + title/company whenever possible.

#### 6. Final CTA
- Reinforce the primary CTA with risk-reduction microcopy.
- E.g.: "Start free. No credit card required."

---

### SEO for Home Page

- **Target keyword:** Brand keyword + main category keyword.
- **Title tag:** `[Brand] | [What you do in 4-6 words]`
- **H1:** Must contain the primary keyword. Can be the same or similar to the headline.
- **Minimum content:** 300-500 words of indexable text (not just images or videos).
- **Internal linking:** The home should link to the site's most important pages.
- **Core Web Vitals:** Copy isn't the only SEO factor — coordinate with the technical team.

---

### Headline Examples — Home Page

❌ "We transform your business with technology"
✅ "The HR software your team will actually use"

❌ "Welcome to [Company]. We're a digital agency."
✅ "More clients from Google. No middleman agency."

❌ "Comprehensive solutions for businesses"
✅ "Financial management for SMBs that don't want to hire a CFO"

## ABOUT PAGE

**Stage:** MOFU
**Objective:** Convert interest into trust. The visitor arrives at the About page with one question: *Can I trust these people?*

### Guiding Principle

The About page is not a company CV. It's a story about why you exist and why that matters to the reader. The most common mistake: talking about the company when the reader wants to know if the company understands them.

**Donald Miller's framework (StoryBrand):** The customer is the hero. The brand is the guide. The About page must make it clear that you understand the customer's world and have the authority to help them.

---

### Recommended Structure — About Page

#### 1. Purpose Statement (not a corporate mission)
- Why the company exists. In human terms, not executive terms.
- What injustice, problem, or gap did they see in the market?

#### 2. The Origin Story
- Brief, honest, specific. Include the conflict that led to creating the company.
- Avoid the heroic version without tension. Vulnerability builds trust.

#### 3. Who We Are (the team)
- Real photos. Full names. One human detail per person (not just the title).
- Focus on the people the client will deal with directly.

#### 4. Values (only if they're real and specific)
- Maximum 4-5 values with a 2-3 line description each.
- ❌ "Integrity, innovation, excellence" — empty and expected.
- ✅ "We don't take clients we can't help" — specific and memorable.

#### 5. Proof of Authority
- Years in the market, number of clients, relevant cases, awards, media mentions.
- Only what's verifiable and meaningful to the buyer persona.

#### 6. Transition CTA
- Don't sell directly from the About. Guide to the next natural step.
- E.g.: "See how we work" → link to services or case studies.

---

### SEO for About Page

- The About page is not an SEO conversion page — don't optimize aggressively.
- **Keyword:** Brand name + city/industry if it's a local business.
- **Title tag:** `About Us | [Brand]` or `About [Brand] | [Brief Descriptor]`
- **Real SEO value:** E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness). Google uses About pages to evaluate site credibility. Include real names, titles, and links to professional profiles.

---

### Opening Examples — About Page

❌ "We are a company founded in 2015 dedicated to offering solutions..."
✅ "We started because we got tired of seeing good products die from bad marketing."

❌ "Our mission is to be leaders in digital innovation."
✅ "We believe no SMB should lose clients for not having a corporation's budget."

---

### Anti-patterns to Avoid (Home + About)

- ❌ Hero with slow-loading background video that says nothing
- ❌ Headline that is the brand slogan without context
- ❌ "We're passionate about what we do" (everyone says it)
- ❌ Team photo without names or context
- ❌ About page that is a corporate timeline without a human story
- ❌ Generic CTA: "Contact us" without specifying what for
