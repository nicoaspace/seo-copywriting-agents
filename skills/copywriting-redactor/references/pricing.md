# Pricing Page

**Stage:** BOFU
**Objective:** Remove pricing friction. The visitor is already considering buying — your job is not to sell the product (the rest of the site already did that), but to justify the value and facilitate the right decision.

---

## Guiding Principle

The pricing page is the most-read and least-worked-on page on most sites. The visitor who arrives here already has intent. The copy needs to convert that intent into action, answering the real question behind the price: *"Is it worth what it costs for someone like me?"*

**David Ogilvy's rule:** "The consumer isn't a moron; she is your wife." Treat her as someone intelligent who needs clear information, not pressure.

---

## Before Structuring: Design Decisions That Impact Copy

Pricing copy can't be designed in a vacuum. These decisions affect what to write:

| Decision | Implication for copy |
|---|---|
| How many plans? | 2-4 plans is the optimal range. More than 4 creates paralysis. |
| Is there a "recommended" plan? | Yes → highlight it visually and in the copy. It's the anchor. |
| Monthly/annual pricing? | Show both. Show the annual savings in absolute terms and %. |
| Is there a free trial? | It's the primary CTA. The price is secondary. |
| Is pricing public or "contact us"? | "Contact us" requires justifying why there's no public price. |

## Recommended Structure

### 1. Page Headline
- Don't use "Plans and Pricing" as the headline. It's a label, not an argument.
- Connect it with value: "The right plan for where you are now" / "Start free. Grow without limits."
- The headline's promise should answer: what will they get in exchange for their money?

### 2. Monthly/Annual Toggle (if applicable)
- If you have annual pricing, show the savings clearly: "Save 2 months" > "20% discount".
- Consider making the annual price the default if that's the one you want them to choose.

### 3. Plan Table

#### Name of Each Plan
- Names should communicate who they're aimed at, not just size.
- ❌ Basic / Pro / Enterprise
- ✅ Freelancer / Team / Business (oriented to the buyer's identity)
- ✅ Starter / Growth / Scale (oriented to business stage)

#### Price
- Show the price per user/month when applicable.
- If it's an annual price, clarify the annual total as well.
- If there's a discount, show the crossed-out price + current price + savings percentage.

#### Brief Plan Description
- 1-2 lines that confirm who this plan is for.
- "Ideal for freelancers managing up to 5 simultaneous projects."

#### Feature List Per Plan
- Start with the most popular or recommended plan as the point of comparison.
- Show the differences clearly — don't repeat everything in each column.
- Convert technical features into benefits whenever possible.

#### CTA Per Plan
- Each plan has its own specific CTA.
- ❌ "Choose" for all plans
- ✅ "Start Free" / "Get Growth" / "Talk to Sales"

### 4. Featured Plan / Anchor Plan
Highlight visually and in copy the plan you most want to sell:
- Label: "Most Popular", "Best Value", "The one most of our clients choose"
- If there's a recommended plan, the feature copy can be more detailed for that one.

### 5. Pricing FAQ Section
The 5 questions you almost always need to answer:
1. Can I change plans later?
2. What happens when my free trial ends?
3. What payment methods are accepted?
4. Are there contracts or can I cancel anytime?
5. Do you have discounts for startups / NGOs / education?

Answer each one honestly and without ambiguity. Ambiguity in pricing creates distrust.

### 6. Guarantee / Risk Reduction
- If you have a money-back guarantee, put it near the CTA, not in the footer.
- Specific: "30-day money-back guarantee, no questions asked" > "satisfaction guaranteed"
- If you have a free trial: "Try 14 days without a credit card" kills the objection before it appears.

### 7. Contextualized Social Proof
- 1-3 testimonials that speak specifically about perceived value or ROI.
- ❌ "Excellent tool, highly recommended."
- ✅ "We pay for the Team plan and in the first month saved 8 hours per week of coordination. It paid for itself."
- If you have case studies with concrete ROI, use them here more than anywhere else.

### 8. Closing CTA
- For those who reached the end without deciding: reduce the barrier to the maximum.
- Repeat the free trial or the entry plan.
- Add a safety microcopy: "No credit card. No contracts. Cancel anytime."

## Pricing Psychology Techniques (use them with judgment, not manipulation)

| Technique | Ethical application |
|---|---|
| **Anchoring** | The most expensive plan on the list makes the mid-tier seem reasonable |
| **Decoy pricing** | A mid plan designed to make the premium look like better value |
| **Monthly framing** | "$X per day" instead of "$X per month" for high prices |
| **Loss aversion** | "Don't lose X hours per week" vs. "gain X hours" |
| **Quantified social proof** | "+5,000 teams already chose the Growth plan" |

> **Note for the writer:** These techniques work best when the product truly delivers on its price. If the copy uses these techniques to sell something that doesn't fulfill its promise, the effect reverses into churn and negative reviews.

## SEO for Pricing Page

The pricing page is not a priority SEO page, but it captures high-intent traffic from keywords like:
- "[product] pricing"
- "[product] plans"
- "[product] how much does it cost"
- "[product] vs [competitor] pricing"

- **Title tag:** `[Brand] Plans and Pricing | Start Free`
- **H1:** Value headline (see section 1 above), not "Pricing"
- **Meta description:** Mention the price range, the free trial, and the main differentiator
- **Structured data:** Consider FAQ schema for the FAQ section questions

---

## Anti-patterns to Avoid

- ❌ "Contact us for pricing" without explaining why there's no public price
- ❌ Plans with generic names that don't say who they're for
- ❌ Fine print hidden about limits or conditions
- ❌ FAQ that doesn't answer the real questions (the ones you get by email every day)
- ❌ Same CTA for all plans ("Choose")
- ❌ Pricing page that requires being logged in to see the prices
- ❌ Money-back guarantee in the footer in 10px font
- ❌ Prices without tax without clarifying it (creates post-checkout friction)
