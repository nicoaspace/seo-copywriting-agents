# Landing Pages & Sales Pages

**Stage:** BOFU (Bottom of Funnel)
**Objective:** One offer. One action. Zero distractions.

---

## Guiding Principle

A landing page is not a website. It's a conversation between the copy and the reader that ends in a decision. Remove everything that doesn't push that decision forward.

**Ogilvy's rule:** "On average, five times as many people read the headline as read the body copy." — If the headline fails, the rest doesn't exist.

---

## Recommended Structure

### 1. Hero Section (Above the fold)
- **Primary headline:** The biggest benefit or the most acute pain. Maximum 10 words.
- **Subheadline:** Expands the headline with the "how" or the "for whom". 1-2 lines.
- **Primary CTA:** Visible without scrolling. Actionable and specific.
- **Quick credential:** Media logo, number of clients, rating. (Optional if space is limited.)

**Recommended techniques:** Benefit-driven headline, numerical specificity, implicit urgency.

### 2. Pain Agitation (The problem)
- Name the buyer persona's pain using their own words.
- Agitate: show the consequence of NOT solving the problem.
- Don't exaggerate — the reader detects manipulation.

**Technique:** PAS (Problem → Agitation → Solution) by Dan Kennedy.

### 3. The Solution
- Introduce the product/service as the natural way out of the problem above.
- It's not a feature catalog — it's a transformation.
- Use the FAB framework: Feature → Advantage → Benefit.

### 4. Social Proof
- Specific testimonials (name, company, concrete result, not generalities).
- Case studies with real numbers.
- Logos of well-known clients if applicable.

**Anti-example:** "Excellent product! Highly recommended." ← No name, no context, no result. Useless.
**Example:** "I went from 2 sales per month to 18 in 6 weeks. I didn't change anything else." — Ana R., marketing consultant, Mexico City.

### 5. Offer and Value Stack (for long sales pages)
- Break down what the offer includes.
- Show perceived value vs. actual price.
- Bonuses if applicable (but only if they're genuinely valuable).

### 6. Objection Handling
- List the buyer persona's 3-5 most common objections.
- Answer them directly, without defensiveness.
- FAQ can go here or after the final CTA.

### 7. Guarantee (if applicable)
- Reduce perceived risk.
- Be specific: "30 days, no questions asked" > "satisfaction guaranteed".

### 8. Final CTA
- Repeat the primary CTA with language reinforced by everything they've read.
- Add a scarcity or urgency reminder only if it's real.

## SEO for Landing Pages

BOFU landing pages have high commercial intent. Optimize for:

- **Intent keywords:** "buy X", "best X for Y", "X pricing", "X vs Y"
- **Title tag:** Include keyword + differentiator. E.g.: "Invoicing Software for Freelancers | Free Trial"
- **H1:** Can be different from the title tag. The H1 is for the reader; the title is for the search engine.
- **Meta description:** Include CTA and keyword. Don't copy the H1.
- **URL slug:** Short, descriptive, with keyword. `/landing/invoicing-software-freelancers`
- **Schema markup:** FAQ schema if you have a frequently asked questions section.

## Techniques by Situation

| Situation | Recommended technique |
|---|---|
| New product, low awareness | Storytelling + education before the offer |
| High price | Value stack + testimonials with concrete ROI |
| Saturated market | Unique differentiator in the headline |
| Cold audience (from ad) | Full PAS + more context before the CTA |
| Warm audience (retargeting) | Direct hero section + offer + quick CTA |
| B2B | Logic + ROI + industry case studies |
| Emotional B2C | Transformation + identity + aspiration |

## Headline Examples (good vs. bad)

❌ "The best project management platform on the market"
✅ "Close projects on time. Without follow-up meetings."

❌ "Innovative solutions for your business"
✅ "Stop losing clients to slow quotes"

❌ "Welcome to [Company Name]"
✅ "Your remote team, finally on the same page"

---

## Anti-patterns to Avoid

- ❌ Full navigation (distracts from the CTA)
- ❌ Multiple CTAs with different destinations
- ❌ Copy in the brand's first person ("We are leaders in...")
- ❌ Paragraphs over 4 lines on mobile
- ❌ Testimonials without a name or specific result
- ❌ "Fill out the form and we'll contact you" as the main CTA
- ❌ Generic stock hero image (people smiling in an office)
