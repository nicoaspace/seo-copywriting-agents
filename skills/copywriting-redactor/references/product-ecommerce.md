# Product Pages — Ecommerce

**Stage:** MOFU → BOFU
**Objective:** Convince the visitor that this specific product is the right choice for their specific situation. The visitor already knows they want something like this — your job is to close the gap between "I'm interested" and "I'm buying it".

---

## Guiding Principle

A product page is not a spec sheet. It's a silent salesperson available 24/7. It must answer every question the buyer would ask in a physical store, anticipate their doubts, and make the purchase process so obvious that the only question left is "when does it arrive?"

**Jeff Bezos's rule:** "We don't make money when we sell things. We make money when we help customers make purchase decisions." Product copy is help, not pressure.

---

## Recommended Structure

### 1. Product Name + Benefit Headline
- The name is the name. But add a subtitle or tagline that communicates the main benefit.
- ❌ "Urban Pro 35L Backpack"
- ✅ "Urban Pro 35L Backpack — Fits everything. Weighs nothing. Passes as carry-on."

### 2. Visual Gallery
- Images sell before the copy does. The copy reinforces what the image initiates.
- The copy should describe what the images can't show: texture, weight, feel, usage context.

### 3. Short Description (above the fold)
- 2-4 lines that capture the essence of the product.
- Speak to the end user, not the generic buyer.
- Include the primary use case and the key differentiator.

### 4. Purchase CTA
- Clear button: "Add to Cart", "Buy Now", "Choose Size".
- Microcopy under the button: shipping info, guarantee, availability.
- Reduce friction with concrete data: "Free shipping in 24h" > "Fast shipping available".

### 5. Long Description — The Salesperson's Body

Use the framework **FAB → usage scenario → objection → proof**:

#### FAB (Feature → Advantage → Benefit)
For each important feature:
- **Feature:** What it has / what it does
- **Advantage:** Why that's better than the alternative
- **Benefit:** What that means for the buyer in their real life

| Feature | Advantage | Benefit |
|---|---|---|
| 5mm neoprene strap | Doesn't heat up or irritate | You can wear it all day without it hurting |
| 10,000 mAh battery | Double the market standard | Two full days without looking for an outlet |
| Ripstop fabric | Resists tears without adding weight | Works just as well in the office as at the airport |

#### Usage Scenario
Paint a real situation where the product makes a difference:
> "It's 6am. Flight in 2 hours. You pack the laptop, charger, day's clothes, and toiletry bag — and everything fits, without forcing any zipper."

#### Anticipate Objections in the Description
The most common ecommerce objections:
- Is it good quality? → Materials, guarantee, manufacturing process
- Will it fit / work for me? → Clear specs, size guide, use cases
- Is it worth the price? → Implicit value comparison, durability, what it replaces
- What if I don't like it? → Prominent return policy

### 6. Technical Specifications
- Table or structured list format.
- Only the specifications relevant to the purchase decision.
- Translate technical specs: not "2000 RPM" but "2000 RPM — enough to mix thick batter without the motor overheating."

### 7. Reviews and Social Proof
- Aggregated score summary if there are many reviews.
- Highlight 2-3 specific reviews that address key objections.
- Reviews with photos > reviews without photos.
- Verified reviews > anonymous reviews.

### 8. Related or Complementary Products
- "Pairs well with..." — contextual cross-selling, not random.
- "Customers who bought this also bought..." — only if it's relevant.

## SEO for Product Pages

Product pages have high transactional intent. Optimize for purchase keywords.

- **Target keywords:** "[product] + buy", "[product] + price", "[product] + deal", "[product] for [specific use]"
- **Title tag:** `[Product Name] | [Differentiator] | [Brand]` — max. 60 characters
- **H1:** Full product name (with variant if applicable: color, size, model)
- **Meta description:** Main benefit + key spec + implicit CTA. Include price if competitive.
- **URLs:** `/products/category/product-name` — no IDs or parameters
- **Structured data (Schema):** Product schema with price, availability, and rating. Essential for rich snippets in Google Shopping.
- **Image alt text:** "[Product Name] [color/variant] — [usage description]"
- **Unique content:** If you sell the same product as 20 other stores, unique copy is your SEO advantage.

## Adaptations by Product Type

| Product type | Copy emphasis |
|---|---|
| High-consideration product (high price) | More copy. Full FAB. Prominent guarantee. ROI or long-term value. |
| Impulse purchase product (low price) | Short copy. Dominant image. Immediate CTA. Real urgency. |
| Technical product (B2B or enthusiast) | Detailed specifications. Compatibility. Professional use cases. |
| Aspirational / lifestyle product | Usage scenario. Identity. Who uses it? In what context? |
| Consumable / recurring | Emphasis on subscription, savings, convenience. |

---

## Anti-patterns to Avoid

- ❌ Manufacturer's description copied without editing (duplicate content + generic)
- ❌ "High quality" without specifying what makes it high quality
- ❌ Feature list without translating to the buyer's benefit
- ❌ Product photos without scale context or real-use context
- ❌ Return policy hidden away (one of the biggest causes of cart abandonment)
- ❌ Purchase CTA more than 2 scrolls away without repetition
- ❌ "More information" as CTA when the user is already on the product page
