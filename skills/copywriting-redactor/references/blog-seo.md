# Blog / SEO Content

**Stage:** TOFU, MOFU, or BOFU — determined by search intent, NOT by the page type.
**Objective:** Rank for a keyword with clear intent, attract the right reader, and move them to the next funnel step.

---

## Step 0 — Determine the Funnel Stage BEFORE Structuring

Blog is not always TOFU. The stage is defined by the intent behind the keyword.

| Search Intent | Stage | Example Keywords | Copy Focus |
|---|---|---|---|
| Learning what something is | **TOFU** | "what is inbound marketing", "how does X work" | Educational, no selling, soft CTA (newsletter, resource) |
| Solving a generic problem | **TOFU** | "how to reduce churn", "why does X fail" | Didactic, empathetic, link to internal resources |
| Evaluating options | **MOFU** | "X vs Y", "best tools for Z", "alternatives to X" | Comparative, honest, consideration CTA (demo, case study) |
| Deciding if it's worth it | **MOFU** | "is X worth it", "X review", "X opinions" | Evaluative, balanced, social proof, direct CTA |
| Ready to buy | **BOFU** | "X pricing", "X for [specific industry]", "buy X" | Persuasive, objections, conversion CTA |

> **Rule:** If the keyword includes a comparison, an evaluation, or a product/competitor name, assume MOFU or BOFU. Not TOFU.

---

## Select the Article Type

Based on the keyword and intent, identify the article type. Each one has its own structure template below.

| Article Type | Intent | Stage |
|---|---|---|
| **Informational / How-to** | Learn how to do something step by step | TOFU |
| **Conceptual / What is** | Understand a term or concept | TOFU |
| **Comparative / X vs Y** | Evaluate two or more options | MOFU |
| **Listicle / Best X** | Find the best options | TOFU–MOFU |
| **Evaluative / Is X Worth It?** | Decide if something is right for them | MOFU |
| **Pillar content / Complete Guide** | Complete reference on a topic | TOFU–MOFU |

## Structure Templates by Article Type

### A. Informational / How-to Article
**Intent:** "how to do X", "steps for X", "guide to X"
**Stage:** TOFU
**Recommended copy formula:** APP (Agree–Promise–Preview) for the intro

```
H1: How to [achieve concrete result] in [time or specific context]
    Ex: "How to Reduce SaaS Churn in Less Than 90 Days"

[INTRO — APP Formula]
Agree: validate the reader's problem or frustration
Promise: what they'll learn exactly
Preview: how many steps, what's included, what's not

H2: Why [the problem exists or why it matters to solve it]?
    2-3 paragraphs of context that justify the tutorial

H2: Step 1 — [Concrete action with verb]
H3: [Sub-step or key concept of step 1]
    Explanation + concrete example

H2: Step 2 — [Concrete action with verb]
H3: [Sub-step or variant]
H3: [What to avoid in this step]

H2: Step 3 — [Concrete action with verb]
    Explanation + example

H2: [Step N...]

H2: Common Mistakes When [Doing X] — optional but high SEO value
H3: Mistake 1 — [Name of the mistake]
H3: Mistake 2 — [Name of the mistake]

H2: Conclusion — [Summary of the benefit of applying these steps]
    1-2 paragraphs
    CTA contextualized to the topic: resource, tool, next article
```

**SEO:** Keyword in H1, in the first H2, and in at least 2 H3s. Length: 1,200–2,500 words.

### B. Conceptual / What Is Article
**Intent:** "what is X", "definition of X", "X meaning"
**Stage:** TOFU
**Recommended copy formula:** APP for the intro, FAB if there's a related product

```
H1: What is [Term]: [Simplified definition or promise of clarity]
    Ex: "What is CAC: How to Calculate It and Why It Defines Your Startup's Health"

[INTRO — APP Formula]
Agree: acknowledge that the term is widely used but poorly understood
Promise: by the end of the article they'll know X concrete thing
Preview: what it covers (definition, calculation, examples, mistakes)

H2: Definition of [Term] — include keyword in H2
    Clear definition in 2-3 sentences, then expand
    Analogy or real-world example if the concept is abstract

H2: How [Term] Works / How to Calculate [Term]
H3: [Formula or mechanism]
H3: [Applied example with real numbers]

H2: [Term] in Practice — real examples
H3: Example 1 — [Specific context]
H3: Example 2 — [Different context or industry]

H2: Difference Between [Term] and [Related term that gets confused]
    Comparison table or explanation of the distinction

H2: Common Mistakes / Myths About [Term] — optional

H2: Conclusion — [Why it matters to understand X]
    CTA: tool, calculator, related guide
```

**SEO:** Keyword in H1 and first H2. Aim for featured snippets with definition in the first 100 words of the body. Length: 800–1,500 words.

### C. Comparative / X vs Y Article
**Intent:** "X vs Y", "X or Y", "difference between X and Y", "[Product A] vs [Product B]"
**Stage:** MOFU
**Recommended copy formula:** BAB for the intro

```
H1: [X] vs [Y]: Which One to Choose in [year or context]?
    Ex: "HubSpot vs Salesforce: Which Is Better for B2B Sales Teams in 2025?"

[INTRO — BAB Formula]
Before: the reader is evaluating and doesn't know which to choose
After: by the end they'll have clear criteria to decide
Bridge: this article compares X criteria with real data, without bias
Tip: declare the verdict quickly if there's a clear winner — MOFU readers appreciate it

H2: Quick Summary — [X] vs [Y]
    Comparison table with the most important criteria:
    | Criterion      | [X]  | [Y]  |
    |----------------|------|------|
    | Price          | ...  | ...  |
    | Ideal for      | ...  | ...  |
    | Ease of use    | ...  | ...  |

H2: What is [X]? — brief, 2-3 paragraphs
H2: What is [Y]? — brief, 2-3 paragraphs

H2: In-Depth Comparison
H3: [Criterion 1 — e.g.: Ease of Use]
    [X does this. Y does this. Key difference:]
H3: [Criterion 2 — e.g.: Pricing and Plans]
H3: [Criterion 3 — e.g.: Integrations]
H3: [Criterion 4 — e.g.: Support and Onboarding]

H2: When to Choose [X]?
    - [User profile or ideal use case]
    - [User profile or ideal use case]

H2: When to Choose [Y]?
    - [User profile or ideal use case]
    - [Alternative if neither fits]

H2: Final Verdict
    1-3 paragraphs with honest conclusion
    If there's a clear winner, say it. If it depends on the case, explain the 2-3 deciding variables.
    CTA: demo, free trial, or case study of the product we promote
```

**SEO:** Keyword "[X] vs [Y]" in H1 and meta description. Table favors featured snippets. Length: 1,500–2,500 words.

---

### D. Listicle / Best X for Y
**Intent:** "best tools for X", "top X of [category]"
**Stage:** TOFU–MOFU
**Recommended copy formula:** APP for the intro

```
H1: The [N] Best [Category] for [Use Case] in [Year]
    Ex: "The 8 Best Project Management Tools for Agencies in 2025"

[INTRO — APP Formula]
Agree: there are too many options and it's hard to choose
Promise: this list is based on [concrete criteria]
Preview: what each review includes, how they were evaluated

H2: Selection Criteria — optional but adds credibility
    Briefly explain why they're on the list

H2: [Tool 1 — Full Name]
H3: What is [Tool 1]?
H3: Key Features
H3: Pricing
H3: Ideal For
H3: What It Doesn't Do Well — honesty = credibility

H2: [Tool 2]
    [same structure]

H2: [Tool N...]

H2: Comparison Table — Quick Summary
    Table with the N options and the most important criteria

H2: Which Is the Best Option for You?
    Segment the recommendation by profile:
    "If you're X, choose A. If you need Y, choose B."

H2: Conclusion
    Contextualized CTA
```

**SEO:** Number in H1 increases CTR. Keyword with year if content is updated annually. Length: 1,500–3,000 words.

### E. Evaluative / Is X Worth It? Article
**Intent:** "is X worth it", "X review", "X opinions", "should I use X"
**Stage:** MOFU
**Recommended copy formula:** PASO for the body; Pros/Cons structure for the central block

```
H1: Is [Product/Service] Worth It? Honest Analysis [Year]
    Ex: "Is HubSpot Worth It for an SMB? Honest Analysis 2025"

[INTRO]
Confirm the reader is evaluating, not just browsing
Anticipate the verdict without spoiling it: "It depends on X and Y — here's the breakdown"
Establish credibility: who evaluated it, how long, what criteria

H2: What is [X] and Who Is It Designed For?
    Brief context. The reader already knows what it is — don't treat them as ignorant.

H2: Pros and Cons of [X]

    [1 sentence of evaluative context]

    ### Pros
    - **[Benefit category]**: [Specific explanation with example or data]
    - **[Benefit category]**: [Specific explanation]
    - **[Benefit category]**: [Specific explanation]

    ### Cons
    - **[Limitation category]**: [Specific explanation — don't sugarcoat]
    - **[Limitation category]**: [Specific explanation]
    - **[Limitation category]**: [Specific explanation]

    **Verdict**: [1-2 sentences with balanced conclusion and direct recommendation]

H2: Who SHOULD Use [X]?
    - [Profile 1 with concrete reason]
    - [Profile 2 with concrete reason]

H2: Who Should NOT Use [X]?
    - [Profile 1 with concrete reason]
    - [Suggested alternative]

H2: Pricing and Plans — How Much Does It Really Cost?
    Not just list prices — include real cost of implementation, add-ons, etc.

H2: Alternatives to [X] — optional, reinforces analysis credibility

H2: Final Verdict
    Direct recommendation. Don't be ambiguous. The reader needs an answer.
    CTA: free trial, demo, or deeper comparative
```

**SEO:** Evaluation keywords: "[product] opinions", "[product] review", "[product] is it worth it". Review Schema if applicable. Length: 1,500–2,500 words.

### F. Pillar Content / Complete Guide
**Intent:** "complete guide to X", "everything about X", broad category keyword
**Stage:** TOFU–MOFU
**Recommended copy formula:** APP for the intro, modular structure with internal links to satellite articles

```
H1: [Topic]: The Complete Guide for [Specific Audience] [Year]
    Ex: "B2B Email Marketing: The Complete Guide for SaaS in 2025"

[INTRO — Extended APP]
Agree: the topic is complex and there's a lot of noise
Promise: this guide covers X, Y, Z with real examples
Preview: table of contents or mention of the main sections

[TABLE OF CONTENTS — mandatory for long guides]

H2: What is [Topic]? — if the term needs a definition
H3: Definition
H3: Why It Matters in [context/year]

H2: [Subtopic 1 — the most fundamental]
H3: [Key Concept 1.1]
H3: [Key Concept 1.2]
H3: Practical Example of [Subtopic 1]

H2: [Subtopic 2]
H3: [Sub-heading 2.1]
H3: [Sub-heading 2.2]

H2: [Subtopic 3 — more advanced or specific]
H3: [Sub-heading 3.1]
H3: [Sub-heading 3.2]

H2: Recommended Tools for [Topic]
    3-5 tools with internal or external link

H2: Common Mistakes in [Topic]
H3: Mistake 1
H3: Mistake 2

H2: Conclusion — How to Get Started with [Topic] Today
    Actionable summary
    CTA to the next natural step in the funnel
```

**SEO:** Primary keyword in H1. Secondary and LSI keywords in H2 and H3. Internal links to satellite articles. Length: 2,500–5,000+ words. Update annually.

## Universal SEO Checklist for Blog

- [ ] Primary keyword in: H1, first 100 words, at least 1 H2, meta description, main image alt
- [ ] Keyword density: 1-2%
- [ ] **Title tag:** 50-60 characters, keyword near the beginning
- [ ] **Meta description:** 150-160 characters, keyword + benefit + implicit CTA
- [ ] Short URL with keyword: `/blog/project-management-tools` ✅
- [ ] Single H1, H2→H3 hierarchy without skipping levels
- [ ] Table of contents for articles >1,500 words
- [ ] Paragraphs maximum 3-4 lines, average sentences <20 words
- [ ] 2-5 internal links to relevant site pages
- [ ] 1-3 external links to authoritative sources

---

## Anti-patterns to Avoid

- ❌ Treating every blog article as TOFU when the keyword is comparative or evaluative
- ❌ Intro that starts with "In today's digital world..." or "In the information age..."
- ❌ Comparatives without a clear verdict at the end
- ❌ Evaluative articles with only decorative pros and cons
- ❌ Generic headers: "More Information", "Important Aspects", "Conclusions"
- ❌ CTA unrelated to the article topic
- ❌ Claims without data, source, or concrete example
- ❌ Listicle with single-line items without development
