# Case Studies

**Stage:** MOFU → BOFU
**Objective:** Provide concrete proof that your product or service delivers results. The reader is evaluating — they need evidence, not promises. A case study turns "we can help" into "here's exactly how we helped someone like you."

---

## Guiding Principle

A case study is not a press release or a self-congratulatory recap. It's a story about your client's transformation, told from their perspective, with specific, verifiable results. The reader should see themselves in the client's shoes and think: "That's exactly my situation."

**The test:** If you remove your product/service from the case study and it no longer makes sense, you've done it right. If the story works without you, you're just narrating — not proving value.

---

## Recommended Structure

### 1. Title / Headline
- Lead with the result, not the client name.
- Include the metric or transformation in the headline.
- The client name can go in the subtitle.

❌ "Case Study: Acme Corp"
✅ "How Acme Corp Reduced Customer Churn by 47% in 90 Days"

❌ "Working with TechStartup Inc."
✅ "From 3 Manual Reports a Week to Real-Time Dashboards — TechStartup's Story"

### 2. Quick Summary / Snapshot (above the fold)
- A scannable overview for readers who won't read the full case study.
- Format as a sidebar, card, or highlighted box:

| Element | Content |
|---|---|
| **Client** | Name, industry, size |
| **Challenge** | 1-sentence problem |
| **Solution** | 1-sentence what you did |
| **Result** | Key metric(s) |
| **Timeline** | How long it took |

### 3. The Challenge (Before)
- Describe the client's situation before working with you.
- Be specific: what was broken, what was the cost, what had they already tried?
- Use the client's own words when possible (direct quotes are gold).
- Connect the challenge to a problem the reader likely shares.

**Technique:** Paint the "Before" state vividly enough that the reader feels the pain. This is the empathy hook.

### 4. The Solution (The Bridge)
- Explain what you did — the specific approach, tools, or strategy.
- Don't just list features. Explain the reasoning behind each decision.
- Show the process: what happened in week 1, month 1, etc.
- Be honest about challenges during implementation. Perfection isn't credible.

**Key question to answer:** "Why this approach and not the obvious alternative?"

### 5. The Results (After)
- Lead with the most impressive metric.
- Use specific numbers: percentages, dollar amounts, time saved, units shipped.
- Compare before vs. after explicitly.
- Include secondary benefits the client didn't expect.
- If possible, include a timeline: "Within 60 days..." or "By Q2..."

❌ "The client saw significant improvement in their metrics."
✅ "Cost per acquisition dropped from $180 to $62. Pipeline velocity increased 3.2x. The team went from 12 manual reports per week to zero."

### 6. Client Quote / Testimonial
- A direct quote from the client that reinforces the result.
- Include full name, title, and company.
- The best quotes are specific, not generic praise.

❌ "Great team, would recommend."
✅ "We'd been trying to fix our onboarding flow for 18 months. They diagnosed the issue in the first week and had a working solution by week three. Our activation rate went from 23% to 61%." — Maria L., Head of Product, SaaSCo

### 7. Key Takeaways (optional)
- 2-3 bullet points summarizing what made this project successful.
- Useful for readers who want to apply the lessons to their own situation.

### 8. CTA
- Connect the reader's situation to the case study: "Facing a similar challenge?"
- CTA should match the funnel stage: "Book a Free Consultation", "See More Results", "Get a Custom Plan"
- Secondary CTA: link to related case studies or the relevant service page.

## SEO for Case Studies

Case studies target branded, problem-specific, and industry-specific keywords.

- **Target keywords:** "[industry] case study", "[service] results", "[problem] solution", "[brand] + [client industry]"
- **Title tag:** `[Result] — [Client/Industry] Case Study | [Brand]`
- **H1:** Result-driven headline with keyword
- **Meta description:** Challenge + result + CTA. Include the metric.
- **URL:** `/case-studies/client-name-or-result-slug`
- **Internal linking:** Link to the service page, related case studies, and relevant blog content.
- **Schema markup:** Article schema. Consider Review schema if it includes a formal testimonial.
- **Content length:** 800-1,500 words. Quality over quantity — but enough substance to rank.

## Adaptations by Industry/Type

| Type | Emphasis |
|---|---|
| B2B SaaS | ROI metrics, time-to-value, integration complexity solved |
| Agency/Consulting | Process transparency, before/after metrics, client relationship |
| Ecommerce | Revenue increase, conversion rate, AOV lift, traffic growth |
| Healthcare/Regulated | Compliance considerations, patient/client outcomes (anonymized) |
| Startup | Speed of implementation, resource constraints overcome |

---

## Anti-patterns to Avoid

- ❌ Vague results: "significant improvement" without numbers
- ❌ All about you, not about the client's transformation
- ❌ No direct client quotes
- ❌ Wall of text without a quick summary for scanners
- ❌ Hiding the results at the bottom — lead with the outcome
- ❌ Stock photos instead of real client imagery or data visualizations
- ❌ No CTA — the reader is warmed up, don't let them leave without a next step
- ❌ Cookie-cutter format that makes every case study feel identical
